About the Book

Survival Japanese for Tourists – Essential Phrases for Traveling in Japan is a practical guide designed for travelers who want to communicate confidently in Japan without studying complex grammar or memorizing unnecessary vocabulary.

This book focuses on real-life travel situations—from greeting locals and ordering food to navigating trains, shopping, and handling emergencies.

You’ll also learn how Japanese politeness works, helping you sound natural, respectful, and confident even with limited language skills.

What You’ll Learn

  • Essential Japanese words and phrases every tourist needs
  • How politeness and tone affect communication
  • Ordering food at restaurants, cafés, and ramen shops
  • Using trains, taxis, and asking for directions
  • Shopping and convenience store Japanese
  • Hotel, ryokan, and accommodation basics
  • Emergency and medical phrases
  • Cultural tips to avoid common travel mistakes

Who This Book Is For

  • First-time visitors to Japan
  • Travelers who want practical Japanese without grammar overload
  • Visitors who want to be polite, respectful, and understood
  • Anyone looking for a simple, real-world Japanese phrasebook
  • Fans who want to recognize everyday phrases heard in anime
